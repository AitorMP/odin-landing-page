Fenrir

Fenrir is the great wolf in Norse Mythology who breaks free from his chains at Ragnarök, the twilight of the gods, kills Odin, and is then killed by Odin's son Vidarr. Fenrir is the son of the trickster god Loki and brother of the World Serpent Jormungandr and the jotunn Hel

Fenrir, also known as Hróðvitnir (fame-wolf), is a Wolf-Giant-god and considered as one of the greatest enemies of the Aesir.

Fenrir facts

In Norse mythology , Fenrir represents savagery that ultimately cannot be controlled, even by the gods.

He was the son of the trickster god Loki and a giantess, Angerboda.

Fenrir has two sons, Hati ('hate') and Skoll.
